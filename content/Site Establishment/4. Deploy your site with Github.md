#### SSH Key Generation:
1. Let's generate ourselves a **SSH** private and public key pair on our device via the terminal:
	```bash
	ssh-keygen -t ed25519
	```
	- Example Output:
	![[Pasted image 20250425230644.png]]
	
>[!Info]- What is ed25519?
>- ed25519 utilizes elliptical curve cryptography based on [Curve25519🤯].
>	- This method ensures consistent, fast, and secure key generation, data signing, and verification.
>	- In comparison to other SSH methods:
>		- ed25519 is faster and more secure than RSA.
>		- es25519 has more consistent performance than ECSDA.

2. Enter a password, or leave blank, and press enter:
	- Next you'll be prompted to enter an optional password:
	- Reasons to enter a password:
		- You're device is not under your sole control (i.e. has multiple users).
		- You're device is at a higher risk of being lost or stolen (i.e laptop).
		- You're paranoid like me 😒
	- Reasons to *NOT* enter a password:
		- You don't have to enter a password to verify use of the SSH verification every time you push changes to GitHub. 
	- Consider which is the most appropriate security/convenience option for your situation.
